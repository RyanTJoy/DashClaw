## DashClaw positioning shift

Stop framing DashClaw as **“agent observability”** and start framing it as:

**“Agent decision infrastructure.”**

That sounds subtle, but it moves you into a much less crowded category.

Right now, if people hear observability, they map you to:

logging
tracing
monitoring
Datadog style tooling

That space will get crowded fast because every framework and cloud provider will bolt on dashboards.

But almost nobody is clearly owning the layer that answers:

Who approved this
Why did the agent decide this
What assumptions existed
What policy allowed it
What evidence proves it later

That is not monitoring.
That is **decision governance**.

## Why this shift reduces competition

Observability tools compete on ingestion, charts, and logs. That becomes a race against bigger companies.

Decision infrastructure competes on:

auditability
policy enforcement
identity
approval workflows
assumption tracking
action lineage

Those are harder to commoditize because they sit closer to risk and accountability.

You already built features that fit this category:

guard policies
assumption drift
open loops
approval gates
verified agent identity
compliance evidence

You are accidentally positioned for a higher value niche than the one your headline implies.

## What the shift sounds like in practice

Instead of:

“Monitor your agents”

Think:

**“Every autonomous action needs a decision trail.”**

Instead of:

“Agent dashboard”

Think:

**“Decision control plane for autonomous systems.”**

Instead of:

“See what agents are doing”

Think:

**“Approve, trace, and prove every agent decision.”**

Same product. Different gravity.

## Strategic effect if you lean into this

Builders adopt you because they need structure.
Teams adopt you because they need accountability.
Enterprise adopts you because they need evidence.

And suddenly you are not competing with logging tools. You are competing in a category that barely exists yet.
